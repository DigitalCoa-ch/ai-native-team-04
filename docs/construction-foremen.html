Construction Foremen

Who They Are

Foremen are the front-line leaders of individual trade teams—plumbers, electricians, structural workers, safety officers. They directly manage crews, assign tasks, and are responsible for the day-to-day execution of work on the ground.

Responsibilities

  • Assigning and overseeing daily tasks for crew members
  • Ensuring work is completed safely and to specification
  • Reporting progress up the chain
  • Coordinating with adjacent trades on shared work areas
  • Identifying and escalating issues immediately
  • Maintaining quality standards on their trade's work

Pain Points

  • Spending time writing reports and taking photos instead of managing crew
  • Being asked about status by supervisors and managers multiple times per day
  • Discovering that another trade's work has blocked or damaged their planned work
  • Finding out too late that materials or information they needed weren't provided
  • Safety issues being reported through informal channels and getting lost
  • No visibility into what other trades are doing in shared spaces

How the System Helps

Simplified Progress Updates

Instead of formal written reports, foremen can upload photos with brief notes. The AI processes these and updates the project's progress view automatically—less paperwork, same information flow.

Early Warning of Blockers

When another trade completes work that affects a foreman's planned tasks (positively or negatively), the system alerts them. Structural team finishes a ceiling frame and mechanical team's route is now clear—or blocked.

Clear Task Assignments

The system surfaces assigned tasks and their status. Foremen can see at a glance what their team should be working on and what's been completed.

Direct Escalation Path

Safety concerns and critical issues can be reported directly through the system and routed immediately to the right people—no more lost phone calls or messages.

Key Workflows

Morning Task Briefing
  1. Check assigned tasks and their current status
  2. Review any alerts related to today's work
  3. Confirm materials and info availability for planned tasks
Progress Reporting
  1. Take photos of completed work
  2. Add brief note describing what was done
  3. Submit through mobile interface
  4. Receive confirmation and any new alerts triggered
Issue Escalation
  1. Document issue with photo and description
  2. Submit through system with severity assessment
  3. Track escalation status and responses
Coordination Check-In
  1. Review what adjacent trades reported today
  2. Identify any conflicts or dependencies
  3. Communicate with relevant foremen to coordinate
